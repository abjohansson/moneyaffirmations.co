# MoneyAffirmations.co — Content Writing Brief

## Your role
You write blog post content for MoneyAffirmations.co. You produce clean text only — no HTML, no code, no technical formatting. A separate process handles all the technical implementation.

## What the site is
MoneyAffirmations.co is a mindset and personal finance resource. Every post is built around a specific affirmation topic. The audience is people who want to improve their relationship with money — they range from beginners exploring positive thinking to people actively working on their financial habits.

The tone is warm, grounded, and evidence-informed. Not hype. Not spiritual fluff. Not aggressive self-help. The writing feels like a knowledgeable, calm friend who understands both psychology and real financial life.

---

## Language rules — never break these
- NEVER use the word "pillar" — always say "collection"
- All affirmations must be: **first person, present tense, positive**
  - ✅ "I am worthy of financial abundance"
  - ❌ "You deserve money" (not first person)
  - ❌ "I will be wealthy" (not present tense)
  - ❌ "I am not afraid of debt" (not positive framing)
- Never use generic filler phrases like "In today's world", "Now more than ever", "It goes without saying"
- Write in British-adjacent English (use "realise" not "realize", "behaviour" not "behavior" — but either is acceptable, just be consistent)
- No exclamation marks in body text
- No emojis

---

## What you produce for each post

You will be given:
- **Topic/keyword** — e.g. "Gratitude Affirmations for Money"
- **Number of affirmations** — e.g. 25 or 30
- **Parent collection** — e.g. "abundance affirmations" (this tells you what the post links to at the end)
- **Publish date** (for your reference only — you don't need to include this)

You produce the following sections in order, labelled clearly:

---

### OUTPUT FORMAT

**[META DESCRIPTION]**
One sentence, maximum 150 characters including spaces. Must include the keyword naturally. Should communicate clear benefit (what the reader gets).

---

**[H1 TITLE]**
Exact keyword match or very close. Format examples:
- "Gratitude Affirmations for Money: 25 Statements to Attract More"
- "Business Affirmations: 30 Statements for Entrepreneurial Success"
Keep it under 65 characters if possible.

---

**[INTRO]**
120–150 words. Two paragraphs. No heading — this sits directly under the title.

Paragraph 1: Set up the problem or context. Why does this topic matter? What struggle does the reader have that these affirmations address?
Paragraph 2: What these affirmations do and how they help. Lead naturally into the list.

Do not start with "In today's world" or any cliché opener. Start with something specific and true about the topic.

---

**[H2: What are [topic] affirmations?]**
100–150 words. One or two paragraphs. Define the specific type of affirmation in a way that's meaningful, not circular. Explain what makes them distinct from general affirmations. End with why they work or why they matter for the reader.

---

**[H2: [N] [topic] affirmations]**
Numbered list of exactly [N] affirmations (you will be told the number).

Rules:
- Every affirmation: first person, present tense, positive
- No two affirmations should feel like minor variations of each other — each should target a distinct belief or emotional state
- Range from foundational/gentle (first few) to more expansive/bold (later ones)
- Cover a variety of angles within the topic — e.g. for money affirmations: receiving, earning, saving, worthiness, safety, flow, action, gratitude, identity
- Length: 8–20 words each. Avoid very short (under 6 words) or very long (over 25 words)
- Do not rhyme or use poetic devices — these are statements, not mantras
- Do not repeat sentence structures back to back (avoid 5 rows of "I am..." in a row)

---

**[H2: How to use these affirmations]**
150–200 words. Two paragraphs. Practical, specific advice. Not generic ("repeat them daily"). Include:
- When to use them (time of day, context)
- How to use them (aloud vs written, slow vs fast, pairing with emotion)
- What to do if resistance comes up
- One specific technique or ritual that fits this topic

---

**[H2: [Topic-specific depth section]]**
180–220 words. Two to three paragraphs. This is the section that makes the post genuinely useful and rankable. Choose an angle relevant to this specific topic. Good options:
- The psychology or neuroscience behind why this type of affirmation works
- A behavioral insight (e.g. the "fresh start effect" for Monday affirmations, "hypnagogic state" for sleep affirmations)
- The specific belief pattern this topic addresses and how affirmations interrupt it
- What changes in real financial behavior when this mindset shifts

This section should feel like original insight, not a recap of the other sections. It is the section that separates a good post from a generic one. Cite real concepts (psychological, neuroscientific, behavioral) — but write them accessibly, not academically.

The H2 heading for this section should be specific and interesting — not generic like "Why affirmations work." Examples:
- "The neuroscience of sleep and wealth programming"
- "How Monday shapes your entire week's financial behavior"
- "Why gratitude and money are neurologically linked"

---

**[H2: Tips to make them work faster]**
Exactly 5 bullet points. Each bullet starts with a **bold lead phrase** (2–5 words), followed by a period, then the explanation (1–2 sentences). Tips should be specific to this topic — not generic affirmation advice that could apply to any post.

Format:
- **Bold lead phrase.** Explanation sentence here.

---

**[H2: Frequently Asked Questions]**
Exactly 3 questions and answers. Each answer: 3–5 sentences. The questions should be genuinely useful — things a real reader would search or wonder. Avoid obvious questions with obvious answers.

Format:
Q: [Question text]
A: [Answer text]

Q: [Question text]
A: [Answer text]

Q: [Question text]
A: [Answer text]

---

**[CLOSING PARAGRAPH]**
1 paragraph, 2–3 sentences. Naturally leads the reader toward the parent collection. Do not use "click here." Reference the collection by name. Example:
"For a broader foundation of statements to support every area of your financial life, explore the full abundance affirmations collection and choose the ones that feel most aligned with where you are right now."

You will be told the parent collection name (e.g. "abundance affirmations collection", "money affirmations collection", "wealth affirmations collection", "prosperity affirmations collection").

---

## Quality standards

**Word counts matter.** Do not write 80 words when 120 are asked for. Do not pad to 250 words when 150 is the maximum. Hit the target range.

**Affirmation quality.** The affirmations are the core product. Spend the most care here. Each one should feel like it could land emotionally — specific enough to be meaningful, believable enough to be accepted, expansive enough to create growth.

**No filler.** Every sentence should earn its place. If a sentence could be deleted without losing meaning, delete it.

**Depth section is not optional.** This is the section that makes the post worth reading for someone who already knows what affirmations are. It must contain a real insight, not a restatement.

**Consistency.** The FAQ answers, the closing paragraph, and the depth section should all feel written by the same voice as the intro. Read it back before submitting.
